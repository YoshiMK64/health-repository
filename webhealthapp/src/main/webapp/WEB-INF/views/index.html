This project was born from conversations in a car, as two GPs travelled to and from work each week.

"Wouldn't it be great to have a 'toolkit' for GPs and other practitioners to be able to access simple techniques and procedures in the mental health field?"

The idea emerged from the growing mental health crisis that seemed endemic to many areas in Australia, and perhaps the world, where next level help from psychologists and psychiatrists was exceedingly more and more difficult to access by GPs and their patients.

The two GPs had previously collaborated on a presentation for GP registrars, aimed at upskilling them with simple effective and evidence based techniques. It seemed to be well received. But an easily accessible tool might be better.

The plan was not to replace the next level care, but to 'hold' distressed patients, while they waited for appointments.

Having said that, some would never be able to afford this next level care and the GP and the care they could offer, would remain their only therapeutic option.
